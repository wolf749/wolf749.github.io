About

Hi, I’m Clayton Anderson and welcome to my website!

I’m a second-year PhD student at UC, San Diego studying Physics. Lately, my physics-computer science background has got me interested in a trio of problems in game design: (1) Procedural generation of terrain, (2) pathfinding on that terrain, and (3) level of detail.

Before I jump into that discussion though, let me include a little more about me…

I did my undergrad at UCSD, graduating cum laude with double majors in Physics [specialization in condensed matter] and Mathematics-Computer Science [one major, two disciplines, a “joint” major]. Additionally, I did two years of undergrad research in The Contentti Group [physical chemistry], won the Malmberg Prize for prowess in experimental physics, and the Dean’s award in Physical Sciences.

I’ve got a passion for teaching [currently a teaching assistant for Mathematical and Computational Physics], and have assisted in a variety of classes in both Math and Physics departments.

My academic career has focused on the physical sciences but my interests are in no way limited to them. I find philosophy and history [currently re-reading Jared Diamond’s Guns, Germs, and Steel, but who isn’t these days?] particularly compelling.

Non-academics leads me to a passion for video games, and moreover, video game design.

Okay! On to terrain, pathfinding, and level of detail!
